# Thesis Intake

## Thesis Seed

- Title: InP photonics as a potential AI optical bottleneck
- Date: March 15, 2026
- Source type: external hypothesis from investor commentary, then checked against company filings and company/industry releases
- Source URL: see `claims-audit.csv`
- Why this matters: AI infrastructure scaling increasingly depends on optical interconnects, and a narrow set of suppliers may control critical upstream layers.

## Raw Claims

1. The InP substrate market is highly concentrated.
2. InP is moving from niche telecom relevance toward broader AI and data-center optical importance.
3. Geopolitical exposure, especially around China-based manufacturing, increases the fragility of this chain.
4. The real bottleneck may sit at substrates, laser capacity, or qualified external light sources rather than at the final module layer.

## Initial Dependency Chain

- End market: hyperscaler AI clusters, optical networking, co-packaged optics, silicon photonics
- System layer: AI networking switches, CPO, optical interconnect systems
- Module layer: transceivers, optical modules, photonics engines
- Component layer: InP lasers, CW laser arrays, external light sources
- Material / substrate layer: indium phosphide substrates and related upstream wafer inputs
- Geography / policy layer: China export-control exposure, non-China capacity expansion, qualification and supply diversification

## Why It Might Be Mispriced

- What the market likely sees: AI networking growth and broad photonics adoption
- What the market may be missing: how narrow the qualified upstream supply chain remains and how fragility may sit one or two layers below the obvious winners
- Why the gap exists: upstream suppliers are obscure, chain reconstruction is tedious, and the value-capture layer is not obvious from headline AI narratives

## Expression Candidates

- Pure-play equity: AXT
- Adjacent beneficiary: Lumentum, Coherent
- Basket / index exposure: broader optical / photonics supplier basket
- Optionality / long-vol angle: only if a later market-data layer shows underpriced variance or cheap long-dated optionality

## Evidence Needed

- Primary-source proof still missing: exact current market-share splits by supplier layer; exact TPU-specific InP dependency
- Capacity data still missing: precise nameplate and qualified output by supplier
- Qualification / substitution data still missing: how quickly customers can switch substrate or laser suppliers
- Pricing / margin evidence still missing: direct proof of allocation behavior or sustained pricing power

## Falsifiers

- The thesis is wrong if: photonics adoption is slower than expected or InP proves less central than the thesis assumes
- The bottleneck is weaker than expected if: current and announced capacity expansions relieve tightness quickly
- The value-capture layer is elsewhere if: device or module vendors monetize scarcity much better than raw-substrate suppliers
