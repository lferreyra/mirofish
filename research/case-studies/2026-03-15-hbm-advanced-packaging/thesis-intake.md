# Thesis Intake

## Thesis Seed

- Title: HBM and advanced packaging as AI infrastructure bottlenecks
- Date: March 15, 2026
- Source type: primary-source synthesis from company releases, company annual reports, and U.S. Department of Commerce / CHIPS releases
- Source URL: see `claims-audit.csv`
- Why this matters: AI accelerator scaling depends on both high-bandwidth memory and the packaging technologies that integrate logic and memory at system scale.

## Raw Claims

1. HBM demand is accelerating rapidly across the AI stack.
2. Qualified HBM supply remains concentrated among a very small number of memory vendors.
3. 2.5D / CoWoS-class advanced packaging capacity is itself a major bottleneck.
4. Advanced substrates and packaging materials are foundational to future packaging scale, even if they are not yet the single tightest point.

## Initial Dependency Chain

- End market: hyperscaler AI clusters, GPUs, ASICs, AI servers
- System layer: accelerator packages, server boards, large-scale AI platforms
- Module layer: HBM-integrated compute packages, heterogeneous packages, chiplet systems
- Component layer: HBM3E / HBM4 stacks, interposers, package integration, underfill and related package materials
- Material / substrate layer: advanced substrates, glass-core or silicon-core substrate technologies, packaging materials
- Geography / policy layer: Asia-heavy packaging concentration, CHIPS-driven domestic packaging investment, long lead times for new qualified capacity

## Why It Might Be Mispriced

- What the market likely sees: HBM is a major AI bottleneck and memory vendors benefit
- What the market may be missing: the packaging layer may be at least as restrictive as raw HBM supply, and some substrate/material bottlenecks may emerge later in the chain
- Why the gap exists: market attention focuses on memory products and GPU demand, while packaging capacity and packaging-material readiness are more technical and easier to overlook

## Expression Candidates

- Pure-play or near-pure-play memory exposure: Micron, SK hynix, Samsung
- Packaging / integration exposure: TSMC, Amkor
- Advanced substrate / materials exposure: Applied Materials and substrate ecosystem buildout proxies
- Optionality / long-vol angle: only if later market-data work shows underpriced variance relative to the bottleneck path

## Evidence Needed

- Primary-source proof still missing: precise current share splits in HBM supply and advanced packaging by layer
- Capacity data still missing: detailed qualified output expansion plans by company and layer
- Qualification / substitution data still missing: how quickly customers can diversify away from current packaging bottlenecks
- Pricing / margin evidence still missing: direct proof of sustained scarcity pricing and not just volume growth

## Falsifiers

- The thesis is wrong if: packaging and HBM capacity expand fast enough to keep up with AI demand without sustained tightness
- The bottleneck is weaker than expected if: qualification broadens and alternative package technologies reduce dependence on the current narrow stack
- The value-capture layer is elsewhere if: system integrators or end customers internalize more of the economics than memory or packaging providers
